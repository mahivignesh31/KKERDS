Course Overview

Welcome to the Essential Computer & IT Skills Training Program! In today's digital age, proficiency with computers and fundamental IT concepts is no longer an option but a necessity. This comprehensive online course is designed for absolute beginners and those looking to refresh their digital knowledge.

You will learn everything from the basics of operating a computer and using essential software like Microsoft Office, to navigating the internet safely and understanding basic cybersecurity principles. Our practical approach ensures you gain hands-on experience, empowering you to confidently use technology for personal, academic, and professional pursuits. Unlock your digital potential and open doors to countless opportunities!

What You'll Learn

Upon successful completion of this program, you will be able to:

  • Confidently operate Windows or macOS operating systems.
  • Create, edit, and format documents in Microsoft Word.
  • Perform calculations and manage data in Microsoft Excel.
  • Design engaging presentations using Microsoft PowerPoint.
  • Navigate the internet safely and efficiently.
  • Manage email accounts and practice proper email etiquette.
  • Understand and apply basic cybersecurity principles.
  • Organize files and folders effectively on your computer and in the cloud.
  • Utilize common productivity and collaboration tools.
  • Identify and troubleshoot basic computer issues.

Prerequisites

This course is designed for beginners. You only need:

  • A computer (desktop or laptop) with internet access.
  • A willingness to learn and explore technology.
  • No prior computer knowledge is required.
  • Basic reading and comprehension skills.

Who This Course Is For

This program is ideal for:

  • Absolute beginners to computers and technology.
  • Students needing essential computer skills for academics.
  • Job seekers looking to enhance their digital literacy for employment.
  • Professionals who want to improve their productivity with office software.
  • Seniors interested in navigating the digital world safely.
  • Anyone aiming to become more confident and competent with technology.

Course Curriculum: Week-Wise Modules

Our structured curriculum breaks down complex IT topics into easy-to-digest weekly modules. Click to expand, watch the video lesson, and explore the learning outcomes for each section.

=

Module Goal: Understand the fundamental components of a computer and learn to navigate its operating system (Windows/macOS).

  • Introduction to Computer Hardware: CPU, RAM, Storage, Peripherals.
  • Understanding Operating Systems: Windows vs. macOS interface, settings.
  • File & Folder Management: Creating, saving, organizing, deleting files.
  • Basic Keyboard Shortcuts & Mouse Usage.
  • Customizing your Desktop: Wallpapers, widgets, taskbar.

Module Goal: Become proficient in creating, editing, and formatting professional documents using Microsoft Word.

  • Word Interface: Ribbons, tabs, basic commands.
  • Text Formatting: Fonts, paragraphs, styles.
  • Inserting Elements: Images, tables, shapes.
  • Page Layout: Margins, headers, footers, page numbers.
  • Proofreading Tools: Spell check, grammar check.
  • Saving & Printing Documents.

Module Goal: Learn to manage data, perform basic calculations, and create simple charts in Microsoft Excel.

  • Excel Interface: Worksheets, cells, rows, columns.
  • Data Entry & Formatting: Numbers, text, dates.
  • Basic Formulas & Functions: SUM, AVERAGE, COUNT, MAX, MIN.
  • Relative & Absolute References.
  • Creating Charts & Graphs: Bar, line, pie charts.
  • Sorting & Filtering Data.

Module Goal: Create impactful and engaging presentations using Microsoft PowerPoint.

  • PowerPoint Interface: Slides, layouts, views.
  • Designing Effective Slides: Text, images, multimedia.
  • Applying Themes & Templates.
  • Transitions & Animations: Enhancing visual flow.
  • Adding Charts, Tables, & SmartArt.
  • Presentation Delivery Tips & Best Practices.

Module Goal: Navigate the internet safely and efficiently, and master professional email communication.

  • Web Browsers: Chrome, Firefox, Edge.
  • Effective Search Techniques.
  • Internet Safety & Security: Phishing, malware, secure browsing.
  • Email Clients & Webmail: Outlook, Gmail setup.
  • Composing Professional Emails.
  • Cloud Storage Basics.

Module Goal: Understand cybersecurity threats and implement basic digital protection measures.

  • Introduction to Common Threats: Viruses, ransomware, phishing.
  • Password Security: Strong passwords, multi-factor authentication.
  • Antivirus & Firewalls.
  • Data Backup & Recovery.
  • Safe Browsing & Online Privacy.
  • Recognizing & Reporting Suspicious Activity.

Module Goal: Gain foundational knowledge of computer networking concepts.

  • What is a Network?: LAN, WAN, Internet.
  • Network Hardware: Routers, switches, modems, cables.
  • IP Addressing & DNS Basics.
  • Wireless Networks: Setup & Security.
  • Basic Troubleshooting Tools: Ping, IPconfig.
  • Introduction to Cloud Computing.

Module Goal: Develop systematic approaches to diagnose and resolve computer & network issues.

  • Troubleshooting Methodology.
  • Diagnosing Hardware Issues.
  • Software Troubleshooting.
  • Printer & Peripheral Troubleshooting.
  • Effective Communication with Users.
  • Using Remote Support Tools.

Module Goal: Learn basic principles of IT project management and tools.

  • Project Management Phases & Methodologies.
  • Project Planning: Scope & Deliverables.
  • Task Management Tools: Trello, Asana basics.
  • Team Collaboration Tools: Slack, Teams basics.
  • Risk Management Basics.
  • Introduction to ITSM.

Module Goal: Prepare for IT certifications, explore career opportunities, and develop a professional plan.

  • Overview of Entry-Level IT Certifications.
  • Building Resume & LinkedIn Profile.
  • Interview Preparation & Networking.
  • Exploring IT Career Paths.
  • Continuous Learning & Skill Development.
  • Personal Branding in IT.
